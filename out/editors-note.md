Welcome to the first issue of **FundRadar East Africa**. The premise is simple: the money didn't disappear this year — the *map* did. With USAID gone and several European donors cutting budgets, the opportunities that remain are scattered across portals nobody has time to check. We check them. Every week, every deadline linked to its primary source.

**Three picks this week:**

1. **Google Africa Applied AI Lab 2026** (deadline 31 Aug) — one of the few big-tech programs currently expanding in Africa rather than retreating. If your organization touches AI-for-development, this is the one to prioritize.
2. **EU FS4Africa Open Call 2** (deadline 31 Aug) — EU–African Union food safety funding aimed at *innovation hubs*, a category many East African agri-tech organizations qualify for without realizing it.
3. **EDCTP3 digital health & AI call** (deadline 2 Sep) — Horizon Europe money specifically for sub-Saharan health research partnerships. Consortium-based, so start partner conversations now, not in August.

**Tender watch:** Uganda has three live World Bank-financed consultancies closing this month — cybersecurity auditing, data protection, and digital skills training. Kenyan readers: the Kakuma/Dadaab baseline assessment closes 28 July.
